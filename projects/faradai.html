FaRADAI

Active 2022–2026 (est.)
🇪🇺 European

Frugal and Robust AI for Defence Advanced Intelligence

Funded by: the European Defence Fund (EDF), 2021 call (EDF-2021-DIGIT-R-2, grant 101103386) — roughly €18.5 million in EU funding over a 42-month duration.

FaRADAI develops frugal and robust AI methods for defence applications — systems that can adapt to new data and situations at reasonable cost, without needing constant retraining or expert intervention. The project combines frugal learning, explainable AI, and multimodal data fusion to support situational awareness and threat assessment across a range of defence use cases. It's coordinated by CERTH/ITI (Greece), with a multi-country European consortium that includes Link Campus University in Italy — where XAIber-Lab contributed as part of the project's AI research work.

About the project

Modern AI methods are often held back in defence contexts by limited or restricted data, since sensitive operational information can't always be shared freely for model training. FaRADAI's frugal learning approach targets exactly this constraint: systems that keep improving from their environment and from user feedback, without needing large fresh datasets or hands-on retraining every time conditions change.

Alongside frugal learning, the project works on robust and explainable detection and identification methods, aiming for models whose outputs a human operator can actually interpret and trust, plus multimodal fusion techniques that combine different sensor and data types into a clearer, unified picture for threat assessment. These methods are being validated across several real-world-inspired defence use cases spanning planning, execution, and decision-making stages.

The consortium spans research institutes, universities, and defence industry partners across many EU member states, coordinated by CERTH/ITI in Greece.
