Tiziano Sammarone

Cyber-Lab

PhD Candidate

Università degli Studi Link — Human Sciences for Innovation, Inclusion & Sustainability, 41st Cycle

Based in Rome, Tiziano works at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and risk management. His PhD, funded by ACN (Italy's National Cybersecurity Agency) and supervised by Prof. Marco Angelini, develops a framework for applying generative AI across the full cyber risk management lifecycle — from identifying and assessing risk through to mitigation and continuous monitoring. A central thread of the work is a bidirectional methodology that maps controls from high-level policy down to technical implementation and traces back up again, paired with a formal notation system built to support that translation. He holds a Master's degree in Cybersecurity (110/110) from Università degli Studi di Roma "La Sapienza", and is a member of XAIber-Lab, where his work spans attack-graph analysis, generative AI tooling, and human-AI collaboration in security decision-making.

[phd topic]

PhD Topic

Integrating Generative AI into the Cyber Risk Management Process: A Human-AI Collaboration Approach

Tiziano's PhD develops a bidirectional methodology for mapping cybersecurity controls from high-level policy down to technical implementation and back, integrating generative AI throughout the cyber risk management lifecycle while keeping a human decision-maker central to the process.
